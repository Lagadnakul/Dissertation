# Dissertation Day 3

## What is Literature Review?

A literature review is the systematic study of existing research related to a topic. It helps researchers understand previous work, identify research gaps, and justify the need for new research.

---

## Why is Literature Review Important?

• Understand current research.

• Avoid duplicating existing work.

• Identify research gaps.

• Learn existing methodologies.

• Build a strong research foundation.

---

## How to Read a Research Paper

For every paper answer:

1. What problem does the paper solve?

2. What methodology is used?

3. What results are achieved?

4. What are the limitations?

5. How is my research different?

---

## Research Gap

A research gap is an important problem that has not been fully solved by previous studies.

My expected research gap:

Existing studies improve code generation and software engineering agents, but limited work focuses on structured self-reflection-based failure recovery using iterative repair for AI coding systems.

---

## Important Learning

A literature review is not copying papers.

It is understanding what researchers have already done and identifying what still needs to be solved.
