# Paper 20

## Title

The 2025 AI Agent Index: Documenting Technical and Safety Features of Deployed Agentic AI Systems

## Authors

Leon Staufer et al.

## Year

2026

---

## 1. Problem Statement

The rapid growth of AI agents has made it difficult for researchers and policymakers to understand how these systems are developed, deployed, evaluated, and governed. Most AI agents provide limited public information regarding safety, transparency, evaluations, autonomy, and accountability.

---

## 2. Methodology

The authors developed the 2025 AI Agent Index by systematically analyzing 30 state-of-the-art AI agents.

The framework evaluates agents across six categories:

- Product Overview
- Company & Accountability
- Technical Capabilities
- Autonomy & Control
- Ecosystem Interaction
- Safety & Evaluation

The study compares chat agents, browser agents, and enterprise workflow agents using 45 evaluation fields per system.

---

## 3. Results

Major findings include:

- Indexed 30 real-world AI agents
- Most agents rely on GPT, Claude, or Gemini models
- Browser agents demonstrate the highest autonomy
- Large transparency gaps exist regarding safety evaluations
- Only a few agents publish agent-specific safety reports
- Enterprise agents provide the least public safety information
- Model Context Protocol (MCP) is widely adopted for interoperability

---

## 4. Limitations

- Relies only on publicly available information
- Internal company evaluations are unavailable
- Excludes domain-specific agents
- Snapshot limited to December 2025
- Rapidly evolving ecosystem may quickly change
